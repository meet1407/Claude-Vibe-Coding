Large Language Models and RAG

Large Language Models (LLMs) are AI models trained on massive text datasets that can understand and generate human-like text. Examples include GPT-4, Claude, Llama, and Mistral.

How LLMs Work:
LLMs are transformer-based models trained with next-token prediction. Given a sequence of words, the model predicts the most likely next word. After training on trillions of tokens, the model develops rich language understanding.

- Tokens: The basic units LLMs process (roughly 4 characters or 0.75 words on average).
- Context Window: The maximum number of tokens an LLM can process at once.
- Temperature: Controls randomness of output. Low temperature = deterministic, high = creative.
- Prompt Engineering: Crafting effective inputs to guide model behavior.

Limitations of LLMs:
- Knowledge Cutoff: LLMs have a training cutoff and don't know recent events.
- Hallucinations: LLMs can confidently generate false information.
- Context Limits: Cannot access information beyond their context window.
- No Private Data: LLMs don't know about proprietary or company-specific information.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG):
RAG solves LLM limitations by connecting the model to an external knowledge base at inference time.

How RAG Works:
1. Ingestion Phase: Documents are split into chunks, converted to embeddings (vectors), and stored in a vector database.
2. Retrieval Phase: When a user asks a question, the query is embedded and compared to stored vectors using cosine similarity.
3. Generation Phase: The most similar document chunks are retrieved and passed as context to the LLM, which generates a grounded answer.

RAG Components:
- Embeddings: Dense vector representations of text that capture semantic meaning.
- Vector Database: Stores embeddings for fast similarity search. Examples: ChromaDB, Pinecone, Weaviate, FAISS.
- Chunking Strategy: How documents are split affects retrieval quality. Common strategies: fixed-size chunks, sentence-based, paragraph-based.
- Reranking: A second-stage model that re-orders retrieved results for better relevance.

Benefits of RAG:
- Reduces hallucinations by grounding answers in retrieved facts.
- Allows LLMs to access up-to-date or private information.
- More cost-effective than fine-tuning for knowledge updates.
- Provides source attribution for answers.
